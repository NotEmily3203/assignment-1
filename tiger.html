Tiger

A tiger is a large carnivorous mammal belonging to the Felidae family and Panthera genus. Known for its distinctive orange fur with black stripes, the tiger is one of the most recognizable and iconic big cats in the world. Tigers have muscular bodies with powerful limbs, allowing them to be agile hunters and formidable predators in their natural habitats. They possess keen senses, including excellent vision and hearing, which aid them in stalking and ambushing prey. Tigers are solitary animals, typically only coming together for mating purposes. They are apex predators, playing a crucial role in maintaining the balance of ecosystems they inhabit. Unfortunately, due to habitat loss, poaching, and human-wildlife conflict, many tiger species are endangered, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts to ensure their survival in the wild.
